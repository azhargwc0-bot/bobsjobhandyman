Garage Door FAQ

Garage Door Repair Questions

Common questions about professional garage door repair services in Virginia Beach.

What are the most common garage door problems?

+

Most common issues include broken springs, opener malfunctions, bent tracks, worn rollers, misaligned safety sensors, remote control problems, and noisy operation. Spring failures and opener issues account for about 70% of all garage door repair calls in Virginia Beach.

Is it dangerous to repair garage door springs myself?

+

Yes, extremely dangerous. Garage door springs are under tremendous tension (150-300+ pounds) and can cause serious injury or death if handled improperly. Professional technicians use specialized tools and safety procedures. This is not a DIY repair.

How do I know if my garage door spring is broken?

+

Signs include: garage door won't open or closes very quickly, door feels extremely heavy, visible gap in torsion spring, door hangs crooked, or loud bang when spring breaks. If you suspect a broken spring, don't attempt to operate the door.

Why won't my garage door opener work with the remote?

+

Common causes include dead remote batteries, remote needs reprogramming, antenna issues, interference from other devices, or opener motor problems. We can diagnose and fix remote control issues quickly, often during the same visit.

What causes garage door tracks to bend?

+

Bent tracks result from impact damage, worn rollers causing binding, improper installation, or attempting to manually lift a door with broken springs. In Virginia Beach, high winds can also cause track damage if the door isn't properly balanced.

How often should garage doors be serviced?

+

Annual maintenance is recommended, especially in Virginia Beach's coastal environment. Salt air accelerates wear and corrosion. Regular service includes lubrication, hardware tightening, balance testing, and safety feature inspection to prevent major problems.

Can you repair all garage door brands and types?

+

Yes, we repair all major garage door brands including Chamberlain, LiftMaster, Craftsman, Genie, Wayne Dalton, Clopay, and others. We work on residential, commercial, single and double doors, all opener types, and specialty doors.

What should I do if my garage door gets damaged in a storm?

+

Don't attempt to operate a storm-damaged door. Disconnect the opener, secure the door if possible, and call for emergency service. We provide storm damage assessment, temporary security measures, and work with insurance companies for repairs.

Why is my garage door making loud noises?

+

Noisy operation typically indicates worn rollers, dry hinges, loose hardware, or track problems. Regular lubrication helps, but worn components need replacement. We can diagnose noise sources and provide effective solutions for quiet operation.

How do coastal conditions affect garage doors?

+

Salt air causes accelerated corrosion of springs, tracks, hardware, and opener components. Humidity affects wood doors and electrical connections. High winds stress all components. We use marine-grade lubricants and corrosion-resistant parts for coastal durability.

When should I replace rather than repair my garage door?

+

Consider replacement if repair costs exceed 50% of replacement cost, the door is over 15-20 years old with multiple problems, significant structural damage exists, or you want to upgrade to modern safety features and smart home integration.

Do you provide emergency garage door repair services?

+

Yes, we offer same-day emergency service for urgent situations like being locked out, security concerns, or storm damage. Emergency service includes temporary fixes to restore security and access, followed by permanent repairs as needed.
