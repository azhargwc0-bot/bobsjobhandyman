Pre-Season Hurricane Checklist: May-June Preparations

The key to hurricane preparedness is starting early. Begin these tasks in May and June, well before the peak hurricane season (August-October):

Exterior Home Inspection

  • Roof Assessment: Check for loose or damaged shingles, missing flashing, and clogged gutters - consider our comprehensive home maintenance services
  • Siding Inspection: Look for loose panels, cracks, or gaps that could allow water infiltration
  • Window and Door Seals: Test all weatherstripping and caulking around openings
  • Foundation Review: Identify any cracks or settling issues that could worsen during storms
  • Tree Maintenance: Trim branches within 10 feet of your home and remove dead trees

Drainage and Water Management

Virginia Beach's flat topography and proximity to the Chesapeake Bay and Lynnhaven River make proper drainage critical:

  • Clean All Gutters: Remove debris and ensure proper water flow to downspouts - our maintenance team can help
  • Inspect Downspouts: Verify they direct water at least 6 feet away from foundation
  • Check Storm Drains: Clear debris from street drains near your property
  • Grade Assessment: Ensure soil slopes away from your home's foundation

Storm-Proofing Your Home: Essential Handyman Tasks

Window Protection Systems

Windows are your home's most vulnerable points during hurricanes. Here are professional-grade protection options:

Plywood Boarding (Budget Option)

  • Use 5/8" exterior-grade plywood minimum
  • Pre-cut and label pieces for quick installation
  • Install with 2.5" galvanized screws every 12 inches
  • Store hardware in labeled bags for each window

Hurricane Shutters (Recommended)

  • Roll-down shutters: Most convenient but highest cost ($15-25 per sq ft)
  • Accordion shutters: Good balance of cost and convenience ($8-15 per sq ft)
  • Colonial shutters: Aesthetic appeal with storm protection ($10-20 per sq ft)
  • Storm panels: Removable aluminum or steel panels ($2-7 per sq ft)

Professional installation recommended - contact our carpentry services team for hurricane shutter installation.

Emergency Home Repair Supply Kit

Having the right tools and materials on hand can mean the difference between minor repairs and major damage. Here's our professional emergency kit checklist:

Essential Tools

  • Cordless drill with extra batteries
  • Circular saw (battery-powered preferred)
  • Hammer (16 oz claw hammer)
  • Utility knife with extra blades
  • Adjustable wrench set
  • Screwdriver set (Phillips and flathead)
  • Level (2-foot and 4-foot)
  • Measuring tape (25-foot)
  • Safety glasses and work gloves
  • Headlamp and flashlights
  • Extension cords (outdoor rated)
  • Plastic sheeting (6-mil thick)
  • Duct tape (multiple rolls)
  • Rope and bungee cords

Materials and Fasteners

  • Plywood sheets (5/8" exterior grade)
  • 2x4 lumber (pressure treated)
  • Galvanized screws (various lengths)
  • Roofing cement and caulk
  • Weatherstripping
  • Sandbags or flood barriers
  • Generator (properly rated for your needs) - we offer generator installation services
  • Fuel stabilizer and extra gas
  • Water pump (for flooding) - our plumbing team can help with installation
  • Tarps (multiple sizes)

Post-Storm Assessment & Immediate Repairs

After a hurricane passes through Virginia Beach, systematic damage assessment is crucial for preventing secondary damage and beginning recovery:

Exterior Assessment Priority Order

  1. Structural Integrity: Check for foundation cracks, wall damage, or roof collapse
  2. Water Intrusion Points: Identify all areas where water entered the home
  3. Roof Damage: Look for missing shingles, damaged flashing, or punctures
  4. Window and Door Damage: Check for broken glass or compromised seals
  5. Siding and Exterior Walls: Identify loose panels or impact damage
  6. Utility Connections: Inspect electrical, gas, and water service connections

When to Call Professional Help

Knowing when to tackle repairs yourself and when to call professionals can save time, money, and prevent injury:

Professional-Only Hurricane Repairs

  • Any electrical work or power line issues
  • Roof repairs above single-story height
  • Structural damage to walls, foundations, or framing
  • Gas line repairs or connections
  • Major plumbing repairs or sewer issues
  • Extensive water damage restoration
  • Hurricane shutter installation - see our carpentry services
  • Tree removal near power lines
