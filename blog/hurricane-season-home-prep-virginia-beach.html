Pre-Season Hurricane Checklist: May-June Preparations

The key to hurricane preparedness is starting early. Begin these tasks in May and June, well before the peak hurricane season (August-October):

Exterior Home Inspection

  • Roof Assessment: Check for loose or damaged shingles, missing flashing, and clogged gutters
  • Siding Inspection: Look for loose panels, cracks, or gaps that could allow water infiltration
  • Window and Door Seals: Test all weatherstripping and caulking around openings
  • Foundation Review: Identify any cracks or settling issues that could worsen during storms
  • Tree Maintenance: Trim branches within 10 feet of your home and remove dead trees

Drainage and Water Management

Virginia Beach's flat topography and proximity to water bodies make proper drainage critical:

  • Clean All Gutters: Remove debris and ensure proper water flow to downspouts
  • Inspect Downspouts: Verify they direct water at least 6 feet away from foundation
  • Check Storm Drains: Clear debris from street drains near your property
  • Grade Assessment: Ensure soil slopes away from your home's foundation

💡 Professional Tip

Install French drains or sump pumps if your home is in a flood-prone area. Many Virginia Beach neighborhoods, especially near the Chesapeake Bay, benefit from these preventive measures.

Storm-Proofing Your Home: Essential Handyman Tasks

Window Protection Systems

Windows are your home's most vulnerable points during hurricanes. Here are professional-grade protection options:

Plywood Boarding (Budget Option)

  • Use 5/8" exterior-grade plywood minimum
  • Pre-cut and label pieces for quick installation
  • Install with 2.5" galvanized screws every 12 inches
  • Store hardware in labeled bags for each window

Hurricane Shutters (Recommended)

  • Roll-down shutters: Most convenient but highest cost ($15-25 per sq ft)
  • Accordion shutters: Good balance of cost and convenience ($8-15 per sq ft)
  • Colonial shutters: Aesthetic appeal with storm protection ($10-20 per sq ft)
  • Storm panels: Removable aluminum or steel panels ($2-7 per sq ft)

Roof Reinforcement

Your roof is your home's first line of defense. Critical reinforcement areas include:

Shingle and Tile Security

  • Replace loose or damaged shingles immediately
  • Install hurricane straps connecting roof trusses to walls
  • Use roofing cement to secure loose shingle edges
  • Install drip edge and ice dam protection

Gutter Reinforcement

  • Install gutter guards to prevent clogging
  • Reinforce gutter hangers with heavy-duty brackets
  • Seal all joints with professional-grade sealant
  • Consider seamless gutters for better storm resistance

⚠️ Safety Warning

Roof work can be extremely dangerous. For repairs above single-story height or complex installations, always hire licensed professionals. Bob's Jobs provides full roof inspection and repair services.

Emergency Home Repair Supply Kit

Having the right tools and materials on hand can mean the difference between minor repairs and major damage. Here's our professional emergency kit checklist:

Essential Tools

  • Cordless drill with extra batteries
  • Circular saw (battery-powered preferred)
  • Hammer (16 oz claw hammer)
  • Utility knife with extra blades
  • Adjustable wrench set
  • Screwdriver set (Phillips and flathead)
  • Level (2-foot and 4-foot)
  • Measuring tape (25-foot)
  • Safety glasses and work gloves
  • Headlamp and flashlights
  • Extension cords (outdoor rated)
  • Plastic sheeting (6-mil thick)
  • Duct tape (multiple rolls)
  • Rope and bungee cords

Materials and Fasteners

  • Plywood sheets (5/8" exterior grade)
  • 2x4 lumber (pressure treated)
  • Galvanized screws (various lengths)
  • Roofing cement and caulk
  • Weatherstripping
  • Sandbags or flood barriers
  • Generator (properly rated for your needs)
  • Fuel stabilizer and extra gas
  • Water pump (for flooding)
  • Tarps (multiple sizes)

💡 Storage Tip

Store all emergency supplies in a waterproof container in an easily accessible location. Label everything clearly and check expiration dates on materials annually.

During the Storm: Safety-First Priorities

When a hurricane hits Virginia Beach, your safety is the top priority. However, there are some immediate actions you can take to minimize damage:

Interior Monitoring

  • Monitor for leaks around windows and doors
  • Place buckets and towels near known weak spots
  • Keep flashlights and battery-powered radio nearby
  • Avoid using candles due to fire risk
  • Stay away from windows and glass doors

Emergency Water Intrusion Response

  • Turn off electricity to affected areas if safe to do so
  • Document damage with photos for insurance
  • Use plastic sheeting to redirect water flow
  • Move valuables to higher, dry areas
  • Never attempt exterior repairs during the storm

⚠️ Critical Safety Reminder

Never go outside during hurricane conditions. Wind speeds can exceed 100 mph, and flying debris can be fatal. Wait for official all-clear signals before venturing outdoors.

Post-Storm Assessment & Immediate Repairs

After a hurricane passes through Virginia Beach, systematic damage assessment is crucial for preventing secondary damage and beginning recovery:

Exterior Assessment Priority Order

  1. Structural Integrity: Check for foundation cracks, wall damage, or roof collapse
  2. Water Intrusion Points: Identify all areas where water entered the home
  3. Roof Damage: Look for missing shingles, damaged flashing, or punctures
  4. Window and Door Damage: Check for broken glass or compromised seals
  5. Siding and Exterior Walls: Identify loose panels or impact damage
  6. Utility Connections: Inspect electrical, gas, and water service connections

Immediate Temporary Repairs

These quick fixes can prevent further damage while you arrange professional repairs:

Roof Leak Temporary Fixes

  • Apply roofing cement to small holes or cracks
  • Cover larger areas with tarps secured with sandbags
  • Use plastic sheeting inside attic to redirect water
  • Clear debris from gutters to restore drainage

Window Protection

  • Cover broken windows with plywood immediately
  • Use plastic sheeting and tape for small cracks
  • Board up any compromised openings to prevent further damage
  • Document all damage with photos before repairs

When to Call Professional Help vs. DIY Repairs

Knowing when to tackle repairs yourself and when to call professionals can save time, money, and prevent injury:

Safe DIY Hurricane Repairs

  • Cleaning debris from gutters (single-story only)
  • Applying weatherstripping to doors and windows
  • Installing window protection (plywood boarding)
  • Minor caulking and sealing work
  • Securing loose siding panels
  • Clearing storm drains and drainage areas
  • Interior water damage cleanup (minor)

Professional-Only Hurricane Repairs

  • Any electrical work or power line issues
  • Roof repairs above single-story height
  • Structural damage to walls, foundations, or framing
  • Gas line repairs or connections
  • Major plumbing repairs or sewer issues
  • Extensive water damage restoration
  • Hurricane shutter installation
  • Tree removal near power lines

Cost Considerations

While DIY repairs save labor costs, consider these factors:

  • Insurance Coverage: Many policies require professional repairs for claims
  • Safety Risks: Injury costs far exceed professional repair costs
  • Code Compliance: Professional work ensures building code compliance
  • Warranty: Professional work includes warranties and guarantees
  • Resale Value: Proper professional repairs maintain home value

Virginia Beach Specific Storm Considerations

Geographic Vulnerabilities

Virginia Beach's unique location presents specific challenges during hurricane season:

Coastal Flooding

  • Storm Surge: Oceanfront properties face 6-12 foot storm surges
  • Back-Bay Flooding: Chesapeake Bay side experiences significant flooding
  • Lynnhaven River: Properties along waterways need special flood preparation

Wind Exposure

  • Oceanfront Winds: Direct Atlantic exposure to hurricane-force winds
  • Funnel Effects: High-rise buildings create wind tunnels
  • Salt Air Corrosion: Coastal environment accelerates material deterioration

Neighborhood-Specific Preparations

Oceanfront/Resort Area

  • Mandatory evacuation zones during major storms
  • Sand infiltration protection needed
  • Hurricane-rated windows essential
  • Elevated foundation considerations

Chesapeake Bay Area

  • Back-bay flooding from storm surge
  • Boat and dock security measures
  • Marsh gas evacuation during storms
  • Waterfront property special concerns

Inland Neighborhoods

  • Tree damage from mature canopy
  • Poor drainage in older developments
  • Power outage duration considerations
  • Access road flooding issues

🌊 Local Knowledge

Virginia Beach flood zones have changed significantly since Hurricane Isabel. Check current FEMA flood maps and consider flood insurance even if not required. Many areas that weren't flood zones in 2003 are now designated flood areas.

Frequently Asked Questions: Hurricane Preparation Virginia Beach

When should I start preparing my Virginia Beach home for hurricane season?

+

Begin preparations in May before hurricane season officially starts June 1st. This gives you time to complete inspections, order materials, and schedule professional services before the rush. Peak season runs August through October, so early preparation is crucial.

How much does it cost to hurricane-proof a Virginia Beach home?

+

Costs vary widely based on home size and current condition. Budget $2,000-8,000 for basic hurricane shutters, $500-2,000 for roof reinforcement, and $1,000-5,000 for drainage improvements. Professional assessment helps prioritize spending for maximum protection.

What type of hurricane shutters work best in Virginia Beach?

+

For Virginia Beach's coastal conditions, accordion shutters or roll-down shutters work best. They resist salt air corrosion and provide quick deployment. Colonial shutters offer aesthetic appeal but require more maintenance. Plywood is budget-friendly but labor-intensive to install.

Should I evacuate or shelter in place during a Virginia Beach hurricane?

+

Always follow official evacuation orders. Oceanfront and low-lying areas typically require evacuation for Category 2+ storms. Inland areas with properly prepared homes may shelter in place for weaker storms, but have evacuation plans ready. Never ignore official evacuation orders.

How do I protect my Virginia Beach home from flooding?

+

Install sump pumps, French drains, and improve yard grading. Elevate utilities above flood level. Use flood barriers or sandbags for temporary protection. Consider flood insurance - many Virginia Beach areas now require it due to changing flood zones since Hurricane Isabel.

What's the best generator size for my Virginia Beach home during hurricanes?

+

A 7,500-12,000 watt generator handles essential systems (refrigerator, lights, sump pump, well pump). Whole-house generators (20,000+ watts) power everything but cost $10,000-15,000 installed. Calculate your actual needs and budget accordingly.

How often should I inspect my hurricane preparations?

+

Conduct full inspections twice yearly - early May and after hurricane season ends. Monthly checks during hurricane season for weather stripping, drainage, and supply kits. After each storm threat, inspect and refresh supplies and check equipment operation.

What should I do immediately after a hurricane hits Virginia Beach?

+

Wait for official all-clear before going outside. Check for gas leaks, electrical damage, and structural issues. Document damage with photos for insurance. Make temporary repairs to prevent further damage. Contact emergency services for serious issues and your insurance company promptly.

Does homeowners insurance cover hurricane damage in Virginia Beach?

+

Standard homeowners insurance covers wind damage but typically excludes flood damage. You need separate flood insurance for storm surge and flooding. Review your policy annually and consider increasing coverage limits. Some policies have separate hurricane deductibles.

How long do hurricane shutters last in Virginia Beach's coastal environment?

+

Quality aluminum shutters last 15-20 years with proper maintenance. Steel shutters need repainting every 5-7 years to prevent rust. Roll-down systems require annual lubrication and inspection. Plywood should be replaced every 2-3 seasons or after any storm use.

What trees should I remove to hurricane-proof my Virginia Beach property?

+

Remove dead, diseased, or damaged trees immediately. Trim branches within 10 feet of your home. Consider removing large trees with shallow root systems near the house. Pine trees are particularly vulnerable to hurricane winds in our area. Consult arborists for mature tree assessment.

Should I turn off utilities before a hurricane in Virginia Beach?

+

Turn off electricity to prevent electrical fires if flooding is expected. Leave gas on unless you smell leaks - turning it back on requires professional service. Keep water on for emergency use unless contamination is reported. Know the location of all utility shut-offs.

How do I prevent mold after hurricane water damage in Virginia Beach?

+

Remove standing water within 24-48 hours. Use fans and dehumidifiers to dry affected areas quickly. Remove water-damaged materials like carpet and drywall. Clean and disinfect all surfaces with bleach solution. Virginia Beach's humidity makes rapid response critical to prevent mold growth.

What's the most common hurricane damage in Virginia Beach homes?

+

Roof damage from wind and flying debris is most common, followed by water intrusion through compromised windows and doors. Flooding from storm surge affects oceanfront and bayside properties. Tree damage to structures and power lines causes extended outages throughout the area.
